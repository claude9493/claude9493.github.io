## Preliminaries
First, Let's make it clear that how micro and macro economics are different.
#### Micronimics
Branch of economics that deals with the behavior of individual economic units--consumers, firms, workers and investors--as well as the markets that thewe units comprise.
 
Microecomics explains how and why these units make econimics decisions.
<!--more-->
#### Macroeconomics
Branch  of economics that deals with aggregate economics variables, such as the level and growth rat eof national output, interest rate, unemployment, and inflation.

## The Themes Of Microeconomics
### Characters

#### 1. Consumers
Consumers have limited incomes, which can be spent on a
wide variety of goods and services, or saved for the future.

Consumers make consumption choices based on preferences, income and prices.
#### 2. Workers
Workers also face constraints and make trade-offs. First,
people must decide whether and when to enter the
workforce. 

Second, workers face trade-offs in their choice of
employment. Finally, workers must sometimes decide how
many hours per week they wish to work, thereby trading off
labor for leisure.

Workers make decisions on work and leisure on the besis of wages.
#### 3. Firms
Firms also face limits in terms of the kinds of products that
they can produce, and the resources available to produce
them.

Firms decide whether to hire more workers or purchase more machines based in part on wage rates and machine prices.

### Markets
Collections of buyers and sellers that together determine the price of a good.

- Background: Market economy, where prices are determined by the interaction of consumers, workers, and firms.( Vice verse there is Centrally planned economy, where prices are set by the government.)

### Positive versus Normative Analysis
- Postive analysis: Analysis *describing relationships of cause and effect*
- Normative analysis: Analysis *examing questions of what ought to be*

## What Is A Market
- Market Definition: Determination of the buyers, sellers, and ranges of products that should be included in a particular market.
- Market definition is important for two reasons:
	- A company must understand who its actual and potential competitors are for the various products that it sells or might sell in the future.
	- Market definition can be important for public policy decisions.
### Arbitrage
Significant difference in the price of a commodity creates a potential for arbitrage: Practice of buying at a low price and selling at a higher price in another.
### Competitive versus Noncompetitive
- Perfectly competitive market: Market with many buyers and sellers, so that no single buyer or seller has a significant impact on price
- Noncompetitive markets: individual firms can jointly affect prices (e.g. World Oil Market, OPEC)

- Market Price: Price prevailing in a *competitive market*.

### The Extent of a Market
- Extent of a market: boundaries of a market, both geographical and in terms of range of products produced and sold within it.

## Real Versus Nominal Prices
- Nominal Price: Absolute price of a good, unadjusted for inflation.
- Real Price: Price of a good relativ e to an aggregate measure of pricel; price adjusted for inflation.


- Consumer Price Index: Meausre of the aggregate price level.
- Producer Price Index: Measure of the aggregate pricce level for intermediate products and wholesale goods.
